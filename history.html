History Of Artificial Intellegence

Alan Turing Publishes: Computing Machinery and Intelligence

During the early 20th century, science fiction familiarized the world with the idea of artificially intelligent robots. This gave birth to a new generation of scientists who were familiar with the topic of artificial intelligence. One such scientist was Alan Turing who published a paper discussing how to build intelligent machines and how to test their intelligence.

1950

John Mcathy coins the term AI

John Mcathy had the objective of creating a robot that had all the properties of the human mind. He believed this was guaranteed to happened within 5-5000 years

1955

Dartmouth Summer Research Project on Artificial Intelligence (DSRPAI) hosted by John McCarthy and Marvin Minsky

Idea finally proofed by Allen Newell, Cliff Shaw, and Herbert Simon’s, Logic Theorist. The logic theorist was considered by many to be the first Ai program, copying a human's problem solving skills. It was funded by Research and Development (RAND) Corporation.
